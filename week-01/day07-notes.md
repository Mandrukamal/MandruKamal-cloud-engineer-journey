# Pricing

## AWS Pricing concepts
1. Pay-as-you-go: Only pay for the resources you use, with no upfront costs or long-term contracts.
2. Pricing models: Choose from various pricing models, including On-Demand, Reserved Instances, and Spot Instances.
3. Free tier: Explore AWS services for free within certain usage limits for 12 months.
4. Cost management: Use tools like AWS Budgets and AWS Cost Explorer to monitor and manage your spending.
5. Pricing calculator: Estimate your costs using the AWS Pricing Calculator to create a customized estimate based on your specific use case.

Pay for what you use.
Pay less when you reserve.
Pay less with volume-based discounts.
Pay even less as AWS grows.

# EC2 purchasing options
1. On-Demand Instances: Pay for compute capacity by the hour or second, with no long-term commitments, unpredictable workloads, and the ability to scale up or down as needed.
2. Reserved Instances: Reserve capacity for 1 or 3 years and receive a significant discount compared to On-Demand pricing.
   Larger upfront payment for a greater discount.
   There are two options.
   Standard Reserved Instances: Provide a significant discount compared to On-Demand pricing.
   Convertible Reserved Instances: Allow you to change the instance type, operating system, or tenancy over the reservation term.
3. Spot Instances: Upto 90% discount. 
    Applications with flexible start and end times
    Urgent computing needs for large amounts of capacity
    Bid on unused EC2 capacity and run those instances for as long as your bid exceeds the current Spot price.
